IMPORTANT NOTE REGARDING FULL RELEASES:

This dataset and all accompanying documentation is the "Full Release"
of waves 1-8 of the Brazilian Legislative Surveys (1990-2017). Includes 
corrections and recoding of variables previously released.

The complete and updated dataset as well as citation information is 
permanently available through this url: https://doi.org/10.7910/DVN/T2PW7F

Previous releases of the data are available for backwards compatibility

        | NOTES: DIAP score of labor progressivism in the ANC, based on 20

kinzo
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

         Kinzo's measure of ideology in the ANC
	 ..................................................................

         1990
	 ..................................................................

         

        | NOTES: Kinzo's measure of ideology in the ANC, based on 11 roll-call
        | votes selected by the author (see Maria D'Alva Gil Kinzo, "O Quadro
        | Partidário e a Constituinte", Revista Brasileira de Ciência Política,
        | vol. 1 no. 1, 1989). This is a 21-point scale ranging from -10 to 10,
        | where the highest score represents the most right-wing position 
        | (thus she correctly labels this as the "escala de conservadorismo").
        | Four of the 11 selected votes in the Kinzo measure also appear among
        | the 20 votes used in the DIAP measure, so the measures are not fully 
        | independent and there is a high correlation between the two 
        | (r = -.892, sig at <.001, N = 561).
        | 
        | The procedure used here to replicate her measure was to score the 
        | conservative position as a 1, the anti-conservative or left position 
        | as a -1, and abstention as a 0 (absences were counted as missing cases,
        | and were not penalized as in the DIAP measure). The denominator was the
        | number of times the individual voted on one of the eleven roll-calls, 
        | so in some cases the measure was calculated on only a handful of votes
        | rather than the maximum of eleven (see Appendix below). The conservative
        | position was a YES vote on four roll-calls (ANC votes 48, 90, 785, and 485)
        | and a NO vote on the remaining seven roll-calls (131, 104, 106, 102, 136, 
        | 533, and 943). Both deputies and senators are included.
